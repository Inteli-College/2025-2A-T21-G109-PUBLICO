# **Executive Summary – Module 14: Financial Model**

## **1. Overview**

This document summarizes the outcomes of **Module 14** of the project **QueryMind**, a startup focused on transforming organizational decision-making through AI-powered analytics agents that interpret Data Warehouse structures and deliver insights via natural language.

The purpose of Module 14 was to **define the financial foundation** of the business — ensuring economic feasibility, pricing sustainability, operational cost structure, and a clear roadmap for funding and performance measurement.

Across **five sprints**, we developed the financial engine behind QueryMind, enabling the transition from validated concept (Module 13) to a viable and scalable business.

---

## **2. Sprint Summaries**

---

### **Sprint 1 – Revenue Model & Pricing Foundations**

**Objective:** Establish how QueryMind will generate revenue and align pricing with market expectations.

**Key Deliverables:**

* Defined QueryMind as a **SaaS B2B platform** with hybrid monetization (subscription + usage-based model).
* Integrated insights from the Module 13 Market Research, where **42.9% of respondents preferred a tiered usage-based pricing model** and **28.6% preferred fixed subscription**.
* Created initial pricing tiers (Essential, Professional, Enterprise) suitable for different company maturities.
* Drafted early revenue projections based on customer adoption scenarios and organizational size.

**Outcome:** A clear, scalable, and market-aligned revenue strategy that balances predictability and flexibility for enterprise clients.

---

### **Sprint 2 – Cost Structure & Operating Model**

**Objective:** Define the financial structure required to build, operate, and scale QueryMind.

**Key Deliverables:**

* Detailed **fixed and variable costs**, including engineering team, cloud infrastructure, vector storage, compliance, and sales.
* Mapped costs specifically related to AI operations (LLM tokens, embeddings, query processing).
* Structured the **operational financial model**, optimized for MVP development and early scalability.
* Identified opportunities to reduce early-stage expenses through cloud credits and hybrid model architectures.

**Outcome:** A comprehensive understanding of cost drivers and a sustainable early-stage operating strategy.

---

### **Sprint 3 – Financial Projections & Viability Modeling**

**Objective:** Build financial projections to assess the business’s long-term feasibility.

**Key Deliverables:**

* Development of **12–24 month projections**, including:

  * MRR and ARR growth
  * CAC and LTV expectations
  * Burn Rate and operational Runway
* Scenario modeling (conservative, moderate, aggressive) based on variable adoption rates.
* Validation that QueryMind can reach financial stability with **15–20 enterprise clients**.
* Alignment of projections with SaaS benchmarks to identify acceptable investor targets.

**Outcome:** Evidence-based confirmation that QueryMind is economically viable under realistic conditions.

---

### **Sprint 4 – Risk Modeling & Sensitivity Analysis**

**Objective:** Identify financial risks and test the resilience of QueryMind’s model under different conditions.

**Key Deliverables:**

* Identified core risks:

  * Computational cost fluctuations (LLMs & cloud)
  * Accuracy concerns impacting retention
  * Long enterprise sales cycles
  * Data security and LGPD compliance requirements
* Performed **sensitivity analyses** on:

  * Pricing elasticity
  * CAC variability
  * Cost-per-query under different infrastructural architectures
* Developed mitigation strategies using caching, hybrid inference pipelines, and governance mechanisms.

**Outcome:** A robust risk framework that reassures stakeholders and prepares QueryMind for realistic operational challenges.

---

### **Sprint 5 – Financing Strategy & Performance Metrics**

**Objective:** Consolidate a funding roadmap and define KPIs to guide business and product performance.

**Key Highlights:**

* **Three-phase financing strategy**:

  1. Bootstrapping + Grants (0–6 months)
  2. Pre-Seed Round (6–12 months)
  3. Seed Round (12–24 months), targeting ARR > R$ 1M
* Defined a complete **KPI ecosystem**, including:

  * **Financial KPIs:** MRR, ARR, CAC, LTV, Runway
  * **Product KPIs:** Accuracy, hallucination rate, response time
  * **Customer KPIs:** Retention, NPS, activation rate
  * **Operational KPIs:** system uptime, cost per query
* Created the **24-month financial roadmap**, bridging MVP development, early adoption, and commercial scaling.

**Outcome:** An investor-ready financial strategy and a clear performance monitoring structure to support upcoming module milestones.

---

## **3. Key Insights**

* A hybrid pricing model (subscription + usage) is ideal for organizations with dynamic analytics needs.
* Cloud and LLM costs must be carefully optimized to achieve sustainable margins as usage grows.
* Market research confirms that users are willing to pay for autonomy, speed, and data democratization — directly supporting QueryMind’s business model.
* Financial viability depends strongly on maintaining **high accuracy and low hallucination rates**, reaffirming the need for robust AI governance.
* The financing strategy positions QueryMind for scalable growth, enabling both product maturity and commercial expansion.
